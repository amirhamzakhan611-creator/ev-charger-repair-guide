Block Descriptions

AC INPUT Input Protection Stage

Fuse (F1): Protects against overcurrent and short circuits. Rating typically 2A-5A.

MOV (Metal Oxide Varistor): Clamps voltage spikes from mains. Shunts surge energy to ground.

NTC Thermistor: Limits inrush current when capacitor is empty. Resistance decreases as it heats up.

RECT Rectification Stage

Bridge Rectifier: Converts AC to pulsating DC using 4 diodes (or integrated bridge).

Bulk Capacitor: Filters pulsating DC to smooth ~320V DC. Typical values: 68µF-150µF @ 400V.

PWM Switching Control Stage

UC384X PWM IC: Generates high-frequency switching pulses (50-100kHz) to drive MOSFET.

MOSFET: Switches high voltage across transformer primary at high frequency.

HF Transformer: Provides galvanic isolation and steps down voltage while stepping up current.

OUT Output Stage

Secondary Rectifier: Fast recovery or Schottky diode rectifies high-frequency AC from transformer secondary.

LC Filter: Toroid inductor + electrolytic capacitor smooths output to pure DC.

Feedback Circuit: TL431 + Optocoupler monitors output voltage and regulates PWM duty cycle.

Output Relay: Disconnects battery when charging complete or fault detected.

🔋 AC to DC Conversion Process

1. Rectification

The bridge rectifier converts the sinusoidal AC waveform into a pulsating DC waveform. Only positive half-cycles pass through, creating voltage peaks at 100Hz (for 50Hz mains).

2. Filtering

The bulk electrolytic capacitor (C11) charges to the peak voltage (~325V) and discharges between peaks, smoothing the pulsating DC.

Typical Bulk Cap Values:
- 68uF / 400V (small chargers)
- 100uF / 400V (standard)
- 150uF / 400V (high power)
Note: 1mF (1000uF) as shown in some diagrams is unusual for primary side.

3. High-Frequency Switching

The PWM controller (UC384X) generates pulses at 50-100kHz to drive the MOSFET. By varying the duty cycle, the output voltage is regulated.

4. High-Frequency Transformation

The HF transformer transfers energy from primary to secondary at high frequency. Because frequency is high, the transformer can be much smaller than a 50Hz transformer of equivalent power.

5. Secondary Rectification

A fast recovery diode or Schottky diode rectifies the high-frequency AC from the transformer secondary. Schottky diodes have lower forward voltage drop (~0.3V) but lower voltage rating.

6. Output Filtering

A toroid inductor (choke) in series with a large electrolytic capacitor forms an LC filter. This removes switching ripple and provides smooth DC to the battery.

7. Feedback Regulation

The TL431 shunt regulator compares a divided sample of output voltage to its 2.5V internal reference. If output rises, TL431 conducts more, increasing optocoupler LED current. The optocoupler transistor pulls down VFB on the PWM IC, reducing duty cycle and lowering output.

Feedback Loop:

Output Voltage UP -> Divider Voltage UP -> TL431 conducts more -> Opto LED brighter -> Opto Transistor conducts more -> VFB Pin 2 voltage DOWN -> PWM reduces duty cycle -> Output Voltage DOWN

(Negative feedback maintains stable output)

🔧 Component Identification & Analysis

F1 Fuse High Risk

Function: Overcurrent protection. Opens circuit when current exceeds rating.

Working: Metal wire melts when I2t energy exceeds limit.

Failure Symptoms: Open circuit, blackened glass, broken wire.

Testing: Continuity mode -> Beep / 0 Ohm = Good | OL = Blown

Common Cause: MOSFET short, bridge short, capacitor short.

MOV1 Metal Oxide Varistor

Function: Surge protection. Clamps voltage transients.

Working: High resistance at normal voltage, becomes conductive above clamp voltage (typically 275V AC).

Failure Symptoms: Burned, cracked, or shorted (0 Ohm).

Testing: Resistance mode -> Should read >1M Ohm | 0 Ohm or low = Failed

NTC NTC Thermistor

Function: Inrush current limiting.

Working: High cold resistance (10-50 Ohm), drops to <1 Ohm when hot.

Failure Symptoms: Open circuit (no power), or stays high resistance (slow start).

Testing: Resistance mode cold -> 10-50 Ohm typical | OL = Open

BR1 Bridge Rectifier High Risk

Function: AC to DC conversion.

Working: 4 diodes in bridge configuration allow both half-cycles to contribute to DC output.

Failure Symptoms: No DC output, low output, or fuse blowing.

Testing: Diode mode across AC-AC, AC-+, AC-- -> 0.4-0.7V one way | 0V both ways = Short

C11 Bulk Capacitor High Risk

Function: Filters rectified DC to smooth voltage.

Working: Stores charge at peak voltage, supplies current between peaks.

Failure Symptoms: Bulging top, leakage, low capacitance, high ESR, open circuit.

Testing: Visual inspection + ESR meter -> ESR <2 Ohm typical | Bulging / High ESR = Replace

Value: Typically 68uF-150uF @ 400V-450V

U5 UC384X PWM Controller

Function: Generates regulated switching pulses.

Working: Oscillator sets frequency, error amplifier compares feedback, PWM comparator sets duty cycle, output driver drives MOSFET gate.

Failure Symptoms: No switching, erratic pulses, no VCC startup.

Testing: Check VCC (Pin 7) = 12-18V, VREF (Pin 8) = 5V, Output (Pin 6) = pulsing.

Q1 MOSFET High Risk

Function: High-frequency switching of transformer primary.

Working: Gate voltage controls drain-source conduction. Switches hundreds of volts at high frequency.

Failure Symptoms: Short (fuse blows), open (no output), or degraded (overheating).

Testing: Diode mode D-S -> OL both ways = Good | 0.2-0.6V = Short

Common Types: 2N60, 4N60, 7N60, 10N60 (600V N-channel)

T1 HF Transformer

Function: Energy transfer, isolation, voltage transformation.

Working: Primary energy stored in core during ON time, transferred to secondary during OFF time (flyback operation).

Failure Symptoms: Open winding (no output), shorted turns (overheating, low output), arcing.

Testing: Resistance mode -> Primary: 1-10 Ohm, Secondary: 0.1-1 Ohm | OL = Open winding

D15 Output Rectifier Diode

Function: Rectifies high-frequency AC from secondary.

Working: Fast recovery or Schottky diode with low reverse recovery time.

Failure Symptoms: Short (low output, overheating), open (no output).

Testing: Diode mode -> 0.3-0.6V one way | 0V both ways = Short

L1 Toroid Inductor (Choke)

Function: Output filtering, ripple reduction.

Working: Opposes rapid current changes, stores energy in magnetic field.

Failure Symptoms: Open (high ripple, noise), shorted turns (overheating).

Testing: Resistance mode -> <1 Ohm | OL = Open

U3 TL431 Shunt Regulator

Function: Precision voltage reference and error amplifier.

Working: Compares REF pin to 2.5V internal reference. Conducts when REF > 2.5V.

Failure Symptoms: Output voltage too high or too low, unstable.

Testing: Check cathode-anode voltage with varying input -> should regulate at ~2.5V on REF pin.

U4 Optocoupler (PC817, etc.)

Function: Isolated feedback from secondary to primary.

Working: LED current controls phototransistor conduction across isolation barrier.

Failure Symptoms: No regulation, output too high, LED not lighting.

Testing: Diode mode on LED side -> ~1.1V one way. Transistor side -> OL both ways (unpowered).

⚠️ Common Fault Analysis

🔴 Fault 1: Charger Completely Dead

Symptoms: No LED, no output, no signs of life.

Primary Suspects:

  • Blown Fuse - Check continuity. If blown, DO NOT just replace - find cause!
  • Open NTC - Measure resistance (should be 10-50 Ohm cold)
  • Dead Bridge Rectifier - Test all 4 diodes
  • Open Startup Resistor - High value resistor (100k-500k) feeding VCC
  • Dead PWM IC - No VCC or no oscillation

Repair: Replace faulty component, verify no shorts before repowering.

🔴 Fault 2: Fuse Blows Immediately

Symptoms: Fuse pops as soon as plugged in.

Primary Suspects:

  • Shorted MOSFET - Most common cause. Drain-Source reads ~0 Ohm.
  • Shorted Bridge Rectifier - AC pins shorted to DC output
  • Shorted Bulk Capacitor - Internal dielectric breakdown
  • Shorted MOV - After surge event, MOV can fail short
  • Transformer Primary Short - Less common but possible

Repair: Use diode/continuity mode to find the short. Replace all damaged components. Check gate drive before installing new MOSFET.

🟡 Fault 3: No DC Output (But Primary Seems OK)

Symptoms: Charger powers on, LED may work, but no voltage at output.

Primary Suspects:

  • Open Secondary Diode - Fast recovery diode failed open
  • Open Output Capacitor - No filtering, but some voltage may still appear
  • Transformer Secondary Open - Winding break
  • Open Output Relay - Contacts not closing
  • Broken PCB Trace - Secondary side trace damage

Repair: Check secondary diode first (most common). Test transformer secondary resistance.

🟡 Fault 4: Output Voltage Too Low

Symptoms: Output present but below rated voltage (e.g., 50V instead of 66.8V).

Primary Suspects:

  • Weak Output Capacitor - High ESR causes voltage drop under load
  • Weak Secondary Diode - High forward voltage drop
  • Feedback Issue - TL431 or optocoupler not regulating properly
  • Transformer Issue - Partially shorted turns reduce output
  • Low Input Voltage - Mains voltage below 200V

Repair: Test capacitor ESR, check feedback circuit voltages, verify TL431 reference.

🟡 Fault 5: Output Voltage Too High

Symptoms: Output exceeds rated voltage (e.g., 80V instead of 66.8V). DANGEROUS for battery!

Primary Suspects:

  • Open Feedback Loop - Optocoupler LED not conducting
  • Dead TL431 - Not conducting to drive optocoupler
  • Open Divider Resistor - Voltage sense divider broken
  • Shorted Optocoupler Transistor - Always conducting, confusing PWM

Repair: Check optocoupler LED with diode mode. Verify TL431 reference pin at 2.5V. Check divider resistors.

🟠 Fault 6: Overheating

Symptoms: Charger gets very hot during operation, may shut down thermally.

Primary Suspects:

  • MOSFET Leakage - Not fully turning off, causing resistive heating
  • High ESR Capacitors - Bulk or output caps heating up
  • Shorted Load - Battery or cable short causing overcurrent
  • Transformer Saturation - Core saturating, excessive current
  • Poor Ventilation - Dust blocked, fan failure (if equipped)

Repair: Check MOSFET switching waveform. Replace old capacitors. Verify load is not shorted.

🟠 Fault 7: Battery Not Charging / Not Detected

Symptoms: Charger output OK but battery does not charge, or charging stops early.

Primary Suspects:

  • Low Output Voltage - Battery voltage higher than charger output
  • Faulty Connector - Bad contact, wrong polarity
  • BMS Protection - Battery BMS blocking charge
  • Relay Not Closing - Output relay stuck open
  • Auto-Cutoff Issue - Charger thinks battery is full

Repair: Verify output voltage > battery voltage. Check connectors. Test with known good battery.

🟢 Fault 8: LED Not Working

Symptoms: Charger works but no status indication.

Primary Suspects:

  • Burned LED - Overcurrent or age failure
  • Open LED Resistor - Current limiting resistor open
  • Bad Solder Joint - LED not making contact
  • Control Circuit Issue - LED driver transistor failed

Repair: Check LED with diode mode (should light dimly). Verify series resistor value.

🛠️ Repair Procedure

⚠️ CRITICAL SAFETY STEPS

1. UNPLUG charger from mains before opening
2. DISCHARGE bulk capacitors with 10k Ohm 5W resistor (hold for 10 seconds)
3. VERIFY 0V across bulk capacitor with multimeter
4. USE isolation transformer if available
5. WEAR safety glasses - capacitors can explode
6. WORK on insulated mat

Step-by-Step Repair Process

Step 1: Visual Inspection

  • Look for burnt components (blackened, charred)
  • Check for bulging capacitors (domed top, leaked electrolyte)
  • Inspect discolored PCB traces (brown/black instead of green/copper)
  • Look for cracked components or broken leads
  • Check for foreign objects or insect damage
  • Smell for burned odor (acrid, distinctive)

Step 2: Document & Photograph

Before removing anything, take clear photos of:

  • Overall PCB top and bottom
  • Any damaged areas close-up
  • Component markings and values
  • Wire connections and routing

Before applying power:

  • Check for solder bridges between adjacent pins
  • Verify component polarity (diodes, electrolytics, ICs)
  • Recheck for shorts with multimeter
  • Verify fuse is installed

Step 7: Power ON with Series Bulb Tester

⚠️ MANDATORY SAFETY TEST

Connect a 60W-100W incandescent bulb in series with the mains input.

  • Bulb OFF or dim = Normal current draw, proceed
  • Bulb FULL BRIGHT = Short circuit still present, DISCONNECT IMMEDIATELY

Step 8: Measure Output Voltage

With series bulb still in place:

  • Measure DC output with multimeter
  • Should read rated voltage (e.g., 66.8V) +/-5%
  • Check for excessive ripple with scope if available

Step 9: Full Load Testing

Remove series bulb, connect to load:

  • Use power resistor or dummy load matching battery impedance
  • Monitor output voltage under load
  • Check for overheating after 30 minutes
  • Verify auto-cutoff if applicable
Repair Complete! Document what was replaced for future reference.

🛡️ Safety Guidelines

⚠️ DANGER: HIGH VOLTAGE PRESENT

The primary side of this charger contains lethal voltages:

  • AC Input: 220-240V AC (can kill)
  • Rectified DC: 310-325V DC (can kill)
  • Capacitor stored energy: Dangerous even when unplugged

Essential Safety Rules

1. Always Discharge Capacitors

Large electrolytic capacitors can store lethal charge for minutes or hours after unplugging.

Method: Connect a 10k Ohm 5W resistor across capacitor terminals for 10 seconds. Verify 0V with multimeter before touching.

2. Use Series Bulb Tester

After repair, ALWAYS power up first time with an incandescent bulb in series with mains.

Why: Limits current to ~0.5A if short exists, preventing further damage and fire risk.

3. Use Isolation Transformer

An isolation transformer separates the device under test from earth-referenced mains.

Benefit: Prevents ground loop shocks. Touching one point won't complete circuit to ground.

4. One Hand Rule

When probing live circuits, keep one hand in your pocket or behind your back.

Why: Prevents current from flowing across your chest (heart path) if you touch live and ground simultaneously.

5. Wear Protection

  • Safety glasses: Capacitors can explode violently
  • Insulated gloves: For handling charged components
  • Non-conductive shoes: Reduce ground path risk
  • Long sleeves: Protect from solder splashes and arcs

6. Work Environment

  • Work on insulated mat or wooden table
  • Keep workspace dry - no drinks nearby
  • Ensure good lighting
  • Have fire extinguisher (Class C) accessible
  • Do not work alone - have someone nearby

7. Know When to Stop

DO NOT proceed if:

  • You smell burning or see smoke
  • You hear arcing or sizzling sounds
  • Components are getting too hot to touch
  • You feel tingling or shock sensation
  • You are unsure about a procedure

When in doubt, seek professional help. Your life is worth more than the charger.
